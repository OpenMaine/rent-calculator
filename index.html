Welcome to the Portland, Maine Allowable Rent Increase Calculator

In November 2020, Portland, Maine passed a new ordinance to limit increases in rent. The purpose of the ordinance is to promote community stability and protect tenants, while ensuring landlords receive a fair return on investment. Full text of the ordinance can be found here.

This calculator is a resource to help tenants and landlords calculate the new allowed yearly rent increase for their unit(s).

Is my apartment or unit covered by rent control?

  • Does your building have more than four units?
  • (If no to above) Does your landlord live in the building?
  • Do you live in a dorm or other school housing, or in a building owned by the Portland Public School system?
  • Is your unit owned or operated by a municipal housing authority (such as Portland Housing Authority)?
  • Is your unit funded or controlled by a government agency, such as Section 8 housing? (Note: this excludes Section 8 vouchers. If you are using a Section 8 voucher, answer "No".)
  • Do you live in an accessory dwelling?
  • Do you live in a church, hospital convent, religious facility, or extended care facility?

See the FAQ, or Section 6-231 of the Ordinance for term definitions and exemptions in more detail.

Required Notice:

Landlords are required to give at least 75 days advance notice of an increase in rent, by sending a signed document to the tenant. The letter must include the date tenancy began, date of rent increase, and justifications for increase.

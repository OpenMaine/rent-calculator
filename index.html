Welcome to the Portland Allowable Rent Increase Calculator

In November 2020, Portland passed a new ordinance to limit increases in rent. The purpose of the ordinance is to promote community stability and protect tenants, while ensuring landlords receive a fair return on investment. Full text of the ordinance can be found here.

This calculator is a resource to help tenants and landlords calculate the new allowed yearly rent increase for their unit(s).

Portland's Rent Stabilization Ordinance Applies To

Rental units in the City limits of Portland, that are NOT:

  • Rental Units owned, operated, or otherwise managed by municipal housing authorities;
  • Accommodations provided in a hospital, convent, church, religious facility, or extended care facility;
  • Dormitories owned and operated by an institution of higher education, or by Portland Public Schools;
  • Rental Units within a building containing 2 to 4 dwelling units, one of which the Landlord currently occupies as their principal residence;
  • Accommodations where the amount of rent charged is either controlled or subsidized by a federal, state, or local governmental agency;
  • Accessory dwelling units.

See the FAQ, or Section 6-231 of the Ordinance for term definitions and exemptions in more detail.

See also the City of Portland's list of frequently asked questions (FAQ) about the ordinance.

* New Total may not reflect the full yearly increase allowed. Factors such as Tax Rate Adjustment, new tenancy, Banked Rent, and Rent Board approved improvements to the unit may each have an impact on the allowed increase. See Section 6-234 of the Ordinance for full details.

* The combined total of allowed increase(s) is capped at 10% per year; amounts above 10% may be "banked" for the following year. See Section 6-235 for more on banking rent increases.

Required Notice:

Landlords are required to give at least 75 days advance notice of an increase in rent, by sending a signed document to the tenant. The letter must include the date tenancy began, date of rent increase, and justifications for increase.
